# VetCard PH Go-To-Market Recovery Plan

**Date:** 2026-05-10  
**Market:** Philippines veterinary clinics  
**Status:** Facebook page created, first broad Facebook ad produced cheap but mostly unqualified inbox activity, qualified clinic-owner ad is now running, and first direct outreach batch is moving through email/phone because page-to-page Messenger is often unavailable.  
**Purpose:** give a concrete recovery plan for getting VetCard from "built but not moving" to first serious clinic pilot.

---

## 0. May 11 alignment update

This plan is still directionally correct. The core positioning is working better than the old software-centered pitch:

> reminder recovery first, clinic management system later.

However, live execution changed three operating assumptions:

1. **Facebook ads can create inbox activity, but cheap messages are not the same as qualified clinic demand.** Count only replies that identify a clinic, role, location, current follow-up workflow, or pilot interest.
2. **Messenger is still the best channel when available, but VetCard PH cannot reliably cold-message clinic pages page-to-page.** Many verified clinic pages show no usable outbound Message button while acting as VetCard PH.
3. **The first pilot ask should start smaller: 10 to 20 selected records, not 20 to 30.** The lower number sounds less like work and matches the current ad/email copy.

Current channel priority:

1. Personal/account Messenger when available and appropriate.
2. Phone/Viber/SMS for local clinics.
3. Email/contact form as a backup or parallel first touch.
4. VetCard PH page comments only if intentionally doing a public-comment strategy.

Do not count public comments, page likes, generic "price?" messages, appointment requests, or pet-owner confusion as clinic traction.

## 1. The blunt diagnosis

The current offer is probably too software-centered.

If the message is mostly:

> VetCard helps vet clinics manage pet records.

Many clinic owners will treat it as optional admin software. That is easy to ignore because they already have paper cards, notebooks, Excel, Messenger, Google Sheets, or staff memory.

The better commercial angle is:

> VetCard helps vet clinics bring pets back for vaccines, deworming, grooming, and follow-up visits.

This turns VetCard from a record system into a repeat-visit recovery system. That matters more because missed follow-ups are directly connected to clinic revenue.

The first objective is not to "get SaaS users." The first objective is to prove this:

> A clinic gives VetCard a small batch of records, VetCard identifies upcoming or overdue pets, reminders are sent, and some owners return to the clinic.

If that happens even once, VetCard has a case study.

## 2. What to stop doing for now

### Stop treating Facebook posts as the main sales channel

Keep posting for credibility, but do not expect organic posts to create B2B sales. Clinic owners are not waiting on Facebook for clinic software. Most will only respond when the offer is direct, specific, and low-risk.

Use Facebook as proof that VetCard is real:

- basic page presence
- screenshots
- short demo clips
- before and after workflow posts
- testimonials later

Do not use it as the primary lead source.

### Stop leading with product features

Do not lead with:

- pet records
- dashboard
- SaaS
- clinic management system
- cloud database
- QR profile
- owner portal

Those can be shown later.

Lead with:

- overdue vaccine clients
- missed deworming schedules
- follow-up reminders
- repeat visits
- less manual Messenger follow-up
- free 2-week pilot

### Stop asking clinics to "try the app"

Trying an app feels like work.

Instead, ask them to let you run a small, low-risk reminder pilot.

Better ask:

> Doc, can I help you test reminders for 10 to 20 vaccine or deworming records for free for 2 weeks?

That is easier to say yes to than:

> Would you like to try our veterinary clinic management SaaS?

## 3. New positioning

### Primary one-liner

VetCard helps vet clinics recover missed vaccine, deworming, and follow-up visits through simple digital records and automatic reminders.

### Short Filipino-friendly version

VetCard helps clinics find pets na due or overdue na for vaccine, deworming, or follow-up, then sends reminders so owners come back.

### What VetCard is in sales conversations

For now, VetCard is:

- a comeback reminder system
- a digital vaccine and deworming tracker
- a lightweight patient follow-up tool
- a done-for-you pilot service

It is not yet sold as a full clinic management system.

### Main promise

> We help you bring back clients who are already in your records but may forget their next visit.

This is stronger than promising generic digitization.

## 4. Ideal first customer profile

Do not chase every vet clinic equally. The first customer should have a clear pain that VetCard can solve quickly.

### Best-fit clinics

Prioritize clinics that have:

- visible Facebook activity
- grooming, vaccines, deworming, or wellness services
- staff answering Messenger inquiries
- public posts about vaccine promos, anti-rabies drives, grooming packages, or pet wellness
- paper cards or manual records
- one branch or a small number of branches
- enough foot traffic to have repeat clients

### Avoid for now

Avoid clinics that are:

- too large and bureaucratic
- already using serious clinic software
- purely emergency or surgery-focused
- inactive online
- hard to contact
- unwilling to share even a small sample workflow

### Best geographic focus

Start local. For Philippines B2B, trust matters. Pick one area and become familiar there.

Recommended first focus:

- nearby city/province where you can visit in person
- Bulacan / Pampanga / Metro Manila edge areas if reachable
- clinics with active Facebook pages, public contact numbers, and at least one reachable private channel

The goal is not nationwide awareness. The goal is one signed pilot.

## 5. The first offer

### Offer name

**VetCard 2-Week Comeback Reminder Pilot**

### Offer summary

VetCard will help the clinic test reminders for a small batch of vaccine, deworming, or follow-up records.

### What the clinic gets

- encode 10 to 20 pet records from a sample list or paper cards
- identify due or overdue reminders
- prepare reminder messages
- send reminders through the agreed channel
- track replies and return visits
- give the clinic a simple result report

### What the clinic needs to provide

Only one of these:

- photo of selected vaccine/deworming cards
- spreadsheet of selected patients
- notebook photo
- Messenger export or typed list
- manual list of pet name, owner name, phone, service, last visit, next due date

### Pilot duration

2 weeks.

### Pilot price

Free for the first 1 to 3 clinics, in exchange for:

- permission to use anonymized results
- short testimonial if useful
- honest feedback call

Do not ask for payment before proof unless the clinic is already strongly interested.

### Paid continuation

After the pilot:

- Starter: PHP 999/month
- Standard: PHP 1,499/month
- Setup/import help: PHP 1,000 to PHP 5,000 depending on record volume

Keep pricing simple at this stage. The first proof matters more than optimizing price.

## 6. What to measure during the pilot

The pilot must produce numbers, not just opinions.

### Required metrics

Track:

- records encoded
- due or overdue pets found
- reminders sent
- replies received
- bookings or walk-ins attributed to reminders
- completed return visits
- estimated recovered revenue

### Simple result format

Example:

> In 14 days, VetCard encoded 20 records, found 12 overdue vaccine/deworming clients, sent 12 reminders, received 5 replies, and helped bring back 2 visits.

Even small numbers can help if the clinic sees the logic.

### Estimated revenue formula

Use simple estimates:

```text
Recovered visits x average service value = estimated recovered revenue
```

Example:

```text
3 recovered visits x PHP 800 = PHP 2,400 estimated recovered revenue
```

If monthly VetCard is PHP 999 and it helps recover even 2 to 3 visits, the pricing becomes easier to justify.

## 8. Outreach volume target

The first 10 clinics are not enough signal.

### Minimum next test

Contact 50 clinics over 14 days.

Daily target:

- 5 new clinics per weekday
- 2 follow-ups per weekday
- 1 in-person visit or phone call attempt per day if practical

### Success benchmark

From 50 clinics, reasonable early expectations:

- 5 to 10 replies
- 2 to 4 discovery conversations
- 1 pilot agreement

If there are zero replies after 50 targeted clinics, the message or contact method is wrong.

If there are replies but no pilots, the offer is wrong.

If pilots start but do not continue, the product or result is weak.

## 9. Outreach sequence

Use a 4-touch sequence. Do not send one message and stop.

### Touch 1: Messenger, SMS, phone, or email

```text
Hi Doc/Team, good day po. Quick question lang po:

How do you currently track pets na due or overdue na for vaccine/deworming? Paper card, notebook, Excel, or Messenger po ba?
```

Lead with the question first. If they answer, then send the pilot offer:

```text
Thank you po. VetCard PH is opening a small free 2-week reminder pilot for a few clinics.

No full migration needed. The clinic can send 10 to 20 selected records, then we help find due/overdue pets and prepare reminder messages for clinic approval.

Would this be useful to test for your clinic?
```

### Touch 2: Follow-up after 2 days

```text
Hi Doc/Team, follow-up ko lang po.

The pilot is small lang: 10 to 20 records, usually vaccine/deworming/follow-up. I help find due or overdue pets, prepare reminders, then report how many replied or came back.

No need to migrate all records. Test lang po muna.
```

### Touch 3: Specific use-case message after 4 to 5 days

```text
Doc/Team, example po:

If a pet had 5-in-1 vaccine last month and the next dose is due soon, VetCard can remind the owner automatically instead of relying on manual checking.

Would it help if I prepared a sample due/overdue list from 10 records for free?
```

### Touch 4: Breakup message after 7 to 10 days

```text
Last follow-up po. I do not want to spam.

If vaccine/deworming reminders are not a priority right now, no problem po. If you want to test later, I can help with a small free pilot using only 10 to 20 records.

Thank you po.
```

## 10. In-person visit script

For local clinics, in-person can work better than cold Facebook messages.

### What to bring

Bring:

- phone or tablet with VetCard screenshots
- one-page pilot sheet
- sample reminder report
- sample digital vaccine card
- your contact info

### Opening line

```text
Hi Doc/Team, I am local and building VetCard. It helps clinics track pets due for vaccines, deworming, and follow-ups.

I am not here to sell a big system today. I just want to ask how you currently remind clients when their pet is due for the next vaccine or deworming.
```

### Discovery questions

Ask:

1. How do you track next vaccine or deworming dates today?
2. Who reminds the pet owner?
3. How often do clients miss their next schedule?
4. Do you use paper cards, notebook, Excel, or Messenger?
5. If I help test reminders for 10 to 20 records for free, would that be useful?

### Close

```text
If you are open, I can run a 2-week test. You can send photos of 10 to 20 vaccine/deworming records. I will encode them, find due or overdue pets, prepare reminders, and report the result.
```

## 11. Discovery call script

Keep calls short: 10 to 15 minutes.

### Goal

The goal is not to demo everything. The goal is to confirm reminder pain and get a pilot batch.

### Call flow

1. Thank them and set context.
2. Ask about current process.
3. Quantify missed follow-ups.
4. Offer the small pilot.
5. Agree on the record batch and channel.

### Questions


```text

```


```text

```

```text
Roughly how many vaccine or deworming clients might miss their next schedule in a month?
```

```text

```

### Pilot close

```text
For the pilot, we only need 10 to 20 records. You can blur anything you are not comfortable sharing. I mainly need pet name, owner contact, service, date done, and next due date.

I will prepare the due/overdue list and reminder messages. Then we track replies and return visits for 2 weeks.
```

## 12. Objection handling

### "We already have paper cards."

Response:

```text
Yes po, VetCard does not need to replace paper on day one. The pilot only helps check which pets are due or overdue and sends reminders, so fewer follow-ups are missed.
```

### "We are busy."

Response:

```text
That is why I am offering to do the setup. You can send photos or a small list. I will encode the pilot batch.
```

### "We do not need a system."

Response:

```text
Understood po. This is not a full system rollout. It is just a small test to see if reminders can bring back vaccine or deworming clients.
```

### "How much?"

Response:

```text
The 2-week pilot is free for a small batch. If it helps, continuation starts at PHP 999 per month.
```

### "Is client data safe?"

Response:

```text
For the pilot, we can use only the minimum information needed for reminders: pet name, owner first name or nickname, contact number, service, date, and due date. We do not need full medical history to test the reminder workflow.
```

### "Send details first."

Response:

```text
Sure po. I will send a short summary. May I also ask what you use now for vaccine/deworming follow-up: paper card, notebook, Excel, or Messenger?
```

Always ask one simple question when sending details. Otherwise the conversation dies.

## 18. Decision rules after 14 days

### If 50 clinics contacted and 0 replies

Problem is likely:

- poor contact quality
- message too long
- message sounds spammy
- wrong channel
- no trust signal

Next move:

- switch to in-person and phone
- shorten message
- mention local area
- include one-page pilot image
- ask one question only

### If clinics reply but do not agree to pilot

Problem is likely:

- offer still feels like work
- trust is low
- pilot ask is unclear
- data concern is blocking

Next move:

- reduce pilot to 10 records
- offer to use dummy/sample records first
- offer an on-site 15-minute setup
- emphasize no full migration

### If clinics agree but do not send records

Problem is likely:

- data transfer feels annoying
- staff are busy
- no internal owner assigned

Next move:

- visit and photograph/encode records yourself with permission
- ask for only 10 records
- give them a checklist
- set a specific time

### If pilot runs but no clients return

Problem is likely:

- record batch was too small
- reminders were sent to weak/old numbers
- service chosen had low urgency
- message copy was weak
- tracking was poor

Next move:

- choose vaccine/deworming due soon, not random old records
- send reminders under clinic identity
- add a scheduling link or Messenger reply prompt
- run a second batch

### If pilot brings back even 1 to 3 visits

Problem becomes sales conversion.

Next move:

- create case study
- ask for paid continuation
- ask for referral to another clinic
- use result in outreach

## 21. What success looks like

### Short-term success

Within 30 days:

- 50 to 100 clinics contacted
- at least 5 to 10 replies
- at least 2 discovery calls
- at least 1 pilot started
- at least 1 measurable result report

### Real traction

VetCard is starting to work if:

- clinics let you handle real reminder batches
- staff understand the value without long explanation
- one clinic pays after the pilot
- a clinic refers another clinic
- the pitch becomes easier after showing results

### Not traction

These do not count as traction:

- page likes
- generic compliments
- people saying "nice idea"
- clinics saying "send details" then disappearing
- screenshots that no clinic uses
- building more features without a pilot

## 22. Final operating rule

Until VetCard has at least one paying clinic, every activity should support one of these outcomes:

1. get a clinic conversation
2. get a pilot record batch
3. send reminders
4. measure return visits
5. convert pilot to paid

If an activity does not support one of those five outcomes, defer it.
