## 2. Best business angle for this deal

Do **not** pitch it as a huge digital transformation.

Pitch it as:

- a **practical branch workflow system**
- starting with **Malolos only**
- designed to reduce paper dependence
- introduced through a **gradual migration approach**

Your strongest business message is:

> “You do not need to fully migrate old records before getting value from the system.”

That directly addresses the likely objection that previously blocked adoption.

---

## 3. Recommended pricing model

For a clinic like this, a simple structure is easier to sell than complex pricing.

## Model A — Setup + monthly support

- one-time setup/customization fee
- recurring monthly software/support fee

This is the cleanest option.

## Model B — Setup + monthly + optional migration package

- one-time setup/customization fee
- recurring monthly software/support fee
- optional paid encoding/migration assistance

This is best if they worry about data encoding effort.

---

## 4. Suggested pricing ranges

These are only working ideas so you have a starting point.

## Conservative pilot pricing

- setup: **PHP 15,000–30,000**
- monthly: **PHP 2,000–5,000**

Use this if:

- you want to lower friction
- you mainly want your first real clinic deployment
- you want a pilot/reference client

## Standard small-clinic pricing

- setup: **PHP 30,000–70,000**
- monthly: **PHP 4,000–10,000**

Use this if:

- you are including customization, onboarding, and active support
- the clinic expects you to respond quickly and refine the system with them

## Assisted migration add-on

- flat migration pack: **PHP 5,000–20,000**, or
- per batch/per record pricing, depending on effort

---

## 5. Recommended first offer

If you want the best chance of landing the deal:

1. present a **pilot-first Malolos rollout**
2. keep the initial scope focused
3. charge a **reasonable setup fee**
4. include a **monthly support fee**
5. keep historical migration as a **separate optional add-on**

This is better than pricing everything into one scary number.

---

## 6. Questions to ask before final pricing

Ask these before locking a final amount:

1. How many staff will use it at the start?
2. How many new patient records are created weekly?
3. Will they encode old records themselves, or do they want help?
4. Do they want Malolos only, or future branch rollout in mind?
5. How much support do they expect during rollout?
6. Do they want any reporting or billing features soon?

---

## 7. Likely objections and how to answer

## “Migration will be hard.”

Answer:

“We can avoid a full migration at the start. The system can begin with new and active patients first.”

## “We are not planning to get a system yet.”

Answer:

“That’s exactly why I’m proposing a manageable pilot instead of a large rollout. It’s a smaller first step.”

## “We are used to paper.”

Answer:

“The goal is not to replace everything overnight. It is to improve the parts that consume time every day.”

## “What if the staff don’t adapt immediately?”

Answer:

“The rollout can start with a small group and a simple workflow, then expand once the team is comfortable.”

---

## 8. What you should avoid promising

- do not promise full paper migration immediately
- do not promise every future feature on day one
- do not promise zero bugs
- do not promise enterprise-grade infrastructure if you are not ready yet
- do not commit to unlimited support without defining scope

---

## 9. Best close for the meeting

A good close is:

> “If you’re open to it, I’d recommend starting with a Malolos pilot focused on pet records, appointments, visits, and preventive tracking. We can keep migration gradual and expand only if the clinic sees value.”

That sounds practical, not pushy.
