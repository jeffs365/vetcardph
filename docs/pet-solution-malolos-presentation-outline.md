# Pet Solution Malolos Proposal Presentation Outline

> **Historical proposal note:** This outline reflects an earlier proposal version and is not the source of truth for the current live product workflow. See `docs/current-state.md` for the active system shape.

Use this as your meeting flow or slide outline.

---

## Slide 1 — Opening

**Title:** Digital Pet Records and Clinic Workflow for Pet Solution Malolos

### What to say

“Thank you for taking the time to review this. I prepared a practical proposal for how Pet Solution Malolos can move from paper index cards to a working digital system without forcing a heavy migration right away.”

---

## Slide 2 — What I understood from our conversation

### Key points

- records are still handled through index cards
- there was a previous system direction before, but it did not continue
- migration seems to have been a concern
- there is no active system plan right now

### What to say

“Instead of proposing a big system change all at once, I designed this around the reality of how the clinic operates today.”

---

## Slide 3 — Current pain points

### Key points

- slow record retrieval
- hard to search by pet or owner
- appointment visibility is limited
- preventive follow-ups are harder to track consistently
- paper records are harder to maintain as volume grows

### What to say

“Even if the current paper process works, it becomes harder to sustain as records accumulate and staff need faster access.”

---

## Slide 4 — Proposed solution

### Key points

- pet records
- owner details
- appointments/calendar
- visit history
- vaccination/deworming tracking
- staff accounts with roles

### What to say

“Phase 1 focuses on the highest-value workflows first, not every possible feature.”

---

## Slide 5 — Why this is low-risk

### Key points

- no forced full migration at the start
- start with new and active patients
- encode older records gradually
- branch-first rollout for Malolos

### What to say

“The system can start helping immediately without requiring the clinic to finish converting every paper card first.”

---

## Slide 6 — Demo flow

If you demo the app, use this order:

1. **Pets list/search**
2. **Pet Profile**
3. **Add/Edit Pet**
4. **Calendar/Appointments**
5. **Visit records**
6. **Vaccination/Deworming tracking**

### What to say

“Let me show you the daily workflow first, not the technical side.”

---

## Slide 7 — Rollout approach

### Key points

- discovery/setup
- pilot with selected cases
- live use in Malolos
- gradual record migration
- future branch expansion if successful

### What to say

“This is intended to be a practical rollout, not a disruptive replacement project.”

---

## Slide 8 — Commercial structure

### Key points

- setup fee
- monthly software/support
- optional migration support

### What to say

“I can structure this as a pilot-first offer so the clinic can evaluate the system with lower risk.”

---

## Slide 9 — Why this matters now

### Key points

- cleaner operations
- better continuity of care
- easier follow-up
- more scalable than paper

### What to say

“This is not just about going digital. It is about making the daily clinic workflow easier and more consistent.”

---

## Slide 10 — Next step

### Key points

- review scope
- confirm pricing
- agree on pilot rollout

### What to say

“If the direction makes sense, the next step is to finalize scope and start with a small pilot.”

---

## Presentation tips

- lead with **clinic pain**, not software features
- avoid sounding too technical
- keep repeating **low-risk, gradual migration**
- do not oversell future features that are not ready yet
- focus on **Malolos first**, then mention future branch expansion only as an option
