# VetCard PH Facebook Ads Audit

**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Scope:** read-only review of active Facebook Ad Center ads and inbox quality.

---

## Summary

The current ads are producing low-cost Facebook activity, but the activity is not yet good clinic traction.

The main active message ad is optimized for **Get more messages** and Meta is treating it as high-performing because it produced many cheap messaging conversations. The actual inbox quality is weak: many conversations look like ad quick-replies, accidental clicks, pet-owner appointment confusion, or people asking generic price/service questions.

This should not be counted as serious VetCard demand.

---

## Active Message Ad Observed

**Goal:** Get more messages  
**Budget:** PHP100/day  
**Duration:** 5 days  
**Spend observed:** PHP348.10  
**Results observed:** 30 messaging conversations started  
**Cost per message conversation:** PHP11.60  
**Reach observed:** 5,327  
**Views observed:** 4,372  
**Audience:** Philippines, age 20-65+, Advantage+ audience on

Current copy:

```text
Vet clinics still using paper index cards? 👀 We’re building VetCard to make pet records digital and easier to manage 🐾 Comment your clinic name if you want to try it.
```

### Diagnosis

The ad is too broad and too soft.

Problems:

- "Vet clinics still using paper index cards?" can attract pet owners, staff, curious people, or accidental responders.
- "digital and easier to manage" sounds like general software, not urgent business value.
- "Comment your clinic name" is not enough qualification.
- Objective is message starts, so Meta optimizes for people likely to click/message, not clinic owners.
- Advantage+ audience is broad across the Philippines and likely expands beyond clinic decision-makers.

The ad is good at producing messages, but not yet good at producing qualified clinic pilots.

## Other Active Page Visit Ad Observed

Another active ad was visible with a **Facebook Page Visits** goal.

Current copy:

```text
Lost vaccine cards are stressful. VetCard PH helps keep pet records easier to find for clinics and pet parents. Would this help your pet routine?
```

### Diagnosis

This copy is more pet-owner oriented. It may be useful later for owner awareness, but it does not support the current B2B goal of getting a clinic pilot.

For the current recovery plan, this should not be treated as a primary traction channel.

---

## Recommended Action

Do not judge VetCard demand from the current inbox.

The next ad should qualify much harder:

```text
For PH vet clinic owners/staff only:

Do you still track vaccine/deworming follow-ups with paper cards, notebooks, Excel, or Messenger?

VetCard is opening a free 2-week reminder pilot for a few clinics.

Send 10 to 20 selected records. We help find due/overdue pets and prepare reminder messages so owners come back.

Comment or message:
Clinic name + city
```

Better CTA:

```text
Message your clinic name + city
```

Do not optimize for generic messages. Optimize the copy for fewer but more qualified replies.

---

## Next Operating Rule

Only count these as meaningful ad leads:

- person gives a clinic name
- person says they own/work at/manage a clinic
- person answers how the clinic tracks vaccine/deworming follow-ups
- person agrees to a 10-record pilot

Do not count:

- "How much?"
- "What services?"
- "Can I book?"
- pet-owner appointment inquiries
- generic page likes
- follower-boost vendor offers

---

## Recommended Ad Test

Run a new small test only after current spend/edit decision:

**Objective:** messages  
**Audience:** Philippines, but copy self-qualifies clinic owners/staff  
**Budget:** small daily budget  
**Duration:** 3 to 5 days  
**Success metric:** qualified clinic replies, not message volume

Minimum benchmark:

- 20 to 30 message starts
- at least 2 clinic-name replies
- at least 1 discovery or pilot conversation

If not met, shift spend away from broad FB ads and toward direct clinic outreach.
